# M·VoW — PRODUCT

## Register

**Product** (with hybrid brand surfaces).

Day-to-day flow is product (daily rituals, timers, settings, dashboard). A handful of onboarding/celebration screens (welcome, weekly celebrate variants) function as brand surfaces — emotional, identity-shaping moments where the design IS the product.

## Users

**Primary:** Uzbek-speaking adults (20-45) who feel their attention is being stolen by social media (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube) and want a discipline partner, not another productivity tracker.

**Context:** Modern professionals, students, parents, religious users. Often Muslim (5-daily prayer rhythm is real, namoz vaqti visible on home dashboard). Phones are with them most of the day. They feel busy and rushed; long forms or 5+ option pickers feel like work.

**Reading mode:** Short bursts. They will not read multi-paragraph mentor speeches. Each screen has 5-15 seconds of attention before they need an action.

## Product purpose

Convert reflexive screen time into conscious, focused work sessions. The app guides users through a daily rhythm:
- **Evening:** draft tomorrow's plan
- **Morning:** confirm + 3 Socratic identity questions
- **Day:** run focused sessions with timers, hard-blocking distractions during sessions
- **End of session:** brief reflection
- **Weekly:** analytics + creative celebration

The app's success is when, after 3-6 months, the user has internalized the mentor's question-asking pattern and no longer needs the app.

## Brand personality — "Intizom do'sti"

The mentor is a **discipline friend** who leads to success, NOT a strict warden, surveillance authority, or punishing tracker.

Voice combines:
- **Wise older friend** speaking warm Uzbek
- **Neurobiologist** (Andrew Huberman) translating science to one-line insights
- **Psychologist** (James Clear) on identity-based habits, motivational interviewing
- **Action-calling friend** who turns empathy into a concrete next step

Tonally: warm, encouraging, validating, then redirecting to one small action. Never lecturing. Always question-driven: "Bugun kim bo'lib uyg'onding?" beats "Wake up earlier."

Religious context allowed and respected (prayer time markers, religious task options) but never forced — works equally well for non-religious users.

## Anti-references

What this app is NOT, what we never want it to feel like:

- **Forest / Flipd** — gamified screen-time apps with cute graphics. Too childish. Our user is adult, serious about their life.
- **Generic SaaS productivity dashboards** — pastel cards, hero metrics, gradient text. AI-slop default.
- **Surveillance dystopia** — red warnings, "you failed" guilt, rigid enforcement aesthetics.
- **Bro-productivity** — David Goggins yelling, militaristic dark mode, "no excuses."
- **Mental health app pastel** — soft purples, breathing circles, therapy-speak.
- **Religious app stereotypes** — ornate calligraphy backgrounds, Quran-recitation vibes for a daily-life app.

## Strategic principles

1. **App must not be the distraction.** Every screen is 5-15 seconds. If users spend more than a minute inside any screen that isn't a session timer, the UX has failed. Settings, profile, calendar — accessed only when needed.

2. **One pivotal question per ritual.** Default to ONE most-important question; deeper inquiry is opt-in ("yana savol kerakmi?"). Time-respecting principle: busy people, not therapy patients.

3. **Brain-activating questions over declarations.** Mentor asks; user discovers. "Hoziroq, 2 daqiqada qaysi qadamni qila olasan?" not "Take a small step."

4. **Conscious action over reflexive action.** Every meaningful step is preceded by a question that engages System 2 thinking. Bypass attempts trigger a question, not a wall.

5. **Celebration > punishment.** Weekly celebrate variants (8 different creative metaphors — mountain, stars, garden, tree, river, dawn, phoenix, key) are emotionally prominent. Failures don't trigger guilt-tripping.

6. **Modern UX brevity.** 5 words per sentence max (mentor voice excepted, but still 2-3 sentences). 3-4 options per question maximum. No subtitles when an icon suffices. Default to LESS.

7. **Long-term: invisible mentor.** After 3-6 months the user has internalized the question pattern. The app gracefully fades from active to passive observer.

## Accessibility & i18n

- **Uzbek (default)**, with 9 language switch ready (UZ/RU/EN/TR/AR/ES/FA/KK + custom).
- **Voice input** everywhere a text input exists (mic icon next to every field). Many users prefer speaking.
- **No condescension.** Don't infantilize. Trust the user.
- **Permissions are one-tap-easy.** Rest day, soft mode, energy-state selector all single-tap, no nested menus.
- **Dark mode only — deliberate choice** (the "vault meets cathedral" mood requires it). Not a "to be safe" choice.

## Surfaces

The app has 28 named preview screens, organized as a narrative journey:
- Onboarding (1-5): welcome → tayyor
- First plan (6): AI-driven routine creation
- First day (7-12): session-start → reflection
- Mentor help (13-15): stuck, urgent-time, chat
- Special contexts (16-17): online-class, add-session
- Daily ritual (18-20): tomorrow-plan, daily-brief, morning-confirm
- Long-term view (21-25): home, profile, notifications, calendar, settings
- Weekly rhythm (26-28): rest-mode, weekly-review, celebrate

Each screen is a 5-15s touchpoint, except active session timers.
